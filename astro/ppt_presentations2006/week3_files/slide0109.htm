Implications of the Virial Theorem:
EKE = - ½ Egr             (“Virial Theorem”)
For an ideal non-relativistic monatomic gas in which “thermal gas pressure”
dominates, then in hydrostatic equilibrium:
The total energy of the system  Etot = EKE+Egr  is then given by
Etot = -EKE       or      Etot = ½ Egr 
Let DEtot be a change in the total energy of the system.      For example, if energy is
lost due to radiation escaping the system into space, then DEtot < 0.  Then also,
Conclusion #3: DEK = -DEtot     The change in kinetic (thermal) energy equals
                                                  -1 times the change in total energy.
                                                   Therefore, if energy is lost and radiated away
                                                   the kinetic (thermal) energy increases!
                                                   The gas heats up as energy is lost!
                                                   The gas heats up as the system contracts.
