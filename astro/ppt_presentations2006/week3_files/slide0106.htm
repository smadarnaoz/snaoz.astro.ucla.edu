Saha Equation for Hydrogen:  In thermal equilibrium a precise ratio exists between the
                                                            density of “neutral atomic hydrogen (electrons bound)
                                                            and the density of “ionized hydrogen” (electrons unbound).
For a pure hydrogen gas, define
N(H) = density of hydrogen atoms
N(H+) = density of hydrogen “ions” (free protons)
N(e) = density of electrons
note: For a pure hydrogen gas,  N(e) = N(H+).
 
The ratio is determined by the balance between “photoionization” of atoms by
photons with energies hn > 13.6 eV, and “recombination” of electrons with protons.
The density of available photons is detemined by Planck’s formula un(T).
Saha’s equation for the ionization balance of hydrogen in thermal equilibrium is:
EH = 13.6 eV
