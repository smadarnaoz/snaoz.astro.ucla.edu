Triple alpha reaction:   Enables a fusion jump from helium to carbon.
4He   +   4He   à   8Be   +   g
              However,    8Be à 4He + 4He     in 2.6 x 10-16 s
At high temperatures (T>108 K) enough 8Be remains for an occasional
8Be   +   4He  à  12C*  +  g
                            12C*  à  12C  +  g
Once carbon is produced, elements can be synthesized up to the iron peak
if the temperature is high enough to overcome the Coloumb barriers.
For example,
4He + 12C à 16O + g
etc.
