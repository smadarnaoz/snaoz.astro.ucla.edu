“Color Temperature”  Tc  of a star is defined such that Bn(Tc) gives the best match
to the spectral shape of the star’s radiation spectrum (e.g. via Wien’s Law).
“Effective Temperature”  Teff  of a star is defined such that given the star’s
luminosity L and radius R, then  L  =  4pR2 sTeff4 .
Stellar Temperatures:
Generally,  Tc » Teff     (because stars are approximate black bodies).
